FDM 3D Printing:
Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) printing is a popular 3D printing technology that uses thermoplastic filaments to create three-dimensional objects layer by layer. FDM printers work by melting the filament and extruding it through a nozzle onto a build platform, where it solidifies and forms the desired shape.

SLA 3D Printing:
Stereolithography (SLA) printing is an additive manufacturing technology that utilizes a liquid resin cured by a UV light source to create intricate three-dimensional objects. SLA printers work by selectively curing layers of resin to build up the desired shape, resulting in high-resolution and precise prints.
